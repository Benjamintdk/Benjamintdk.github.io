1st place at NTU IEEE v6.0 Hackathon

Date posted: Sept 2019

This was my first hackathon, so it was quite a novel experience for me and my team to churn out a prototype in 24 hours. We built Litebox, an Android application paired with a controlled light box that could detect if a plate was clean or dirty. We used Android Studios, Tensorflow Lite and Arduino with an ultrasonic sensor to bring the idea to life. I was personally involved in data collection, model training (fine-tuning) and integration with the application. I was pleased as it was my first time training and deploying a CNN model for a serious application.

Although we won first place, there were improvements to be made. Firstly, the model was not sufficiently robust even under the controlled environment of the light box. This was likely due to a miserly dataset. Secondly, the prototype was very crude as we only had 24 hours to build it, and hence the components were shabbily integrated. Given time, we would have used a full on-device solution, perhaps using a Raspberry Pi instead, potentially adding more sensors or an ArduCam, and allowing for on device inference instead.
